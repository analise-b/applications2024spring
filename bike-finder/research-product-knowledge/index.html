Summary:

When purchasing a bike, a buyer will be faced with a lot of questions: what frame of bike do I want? what type of bike is best for me? what will I use my bike for? how often will I use my bike? where do I need my bike to be go/what terrain will it be subjected to? how much do I want to spend on my bike? what colour do I want my bike to be? what features do I want my bike to have? These are just a few of the big questions identified from the different sources, and my own experience looking for a bike. Buyers will have different priorities depending on what they plan to use the bike for. If they are using it for commuting, they may care more about its durability and ability to hold items such as a bag. If they are using it for road racing, they may prioritise its ability for speed and prefer a narrow frame over a more bulky one. If they are planning to use it in a wet/damp environment, they may prioritise the type of frame material of their bike over other features. Through this research, I came across a lot of new information. Previously I did not understand the sheer number of bikes that existed, or how they differed from one another. After reading through these sources, I have a better understanding of the different bikes. I understand now the pros/cons of the different bike frames (and why different bike frames may impact someone's choice). I understand better the complex nature of buying a bike; there are a lot of revolving components that go into purchasing a bike.
